

# 🧬 BioSearch

**South Africa as an Astrobiology Analog Framework for Planetary Biosignature Evolution**

BioSearch is a computational model that classifies planetary environments into evolutionary biosphere phases using geological and geochemical analogs from early Earth, particularly the Barberton Greenstone Belt and the Transvaal Basin.

The system does **not detect life directly**.
Instead, it estimates:

> How a planet’s biosphere *would evolve* and how detectable its biosignatures would be at that stage.

---

# 🌍 Core Idea

Earth’s early history shows that life and planetary conditions co-evolve through distinct stages:

* Hydrothermal emergence systems (Barberton-like)
* Stable marine biospheres
* Atmospheric oxygen transitions (Transvaal-like)
* Terrestrial biosphere expansion

BioSearch encodes this progression into a **phase-based planetary classifier**.

---

# 🧪 Input Parameters

Each planet is described using simplified geophysical variables:

* **Geothermal Activity (0–10)**
  Internal heat driving hydrothermal systems

* **Ocean Presence (True/False)**
  Availability of liquid water environments

* **Atmosphere Type (reducing / neutral / oxidizing)**
  Atmospheric chemical state and evolutionary stage

* **Silica Abundance (0–10)**
  Preservation potential for biosignatures in rocks

* **Tectonic Activity (0–10)**
  Surface recycling rate affecting fossil preservation

* **Stellar Radiation (0–10)**
  Environmental stress and signal degradation

* **Planet Age (relative scale)**
  Controls long-term atmospheric and biosphere evolution

---

# 🧭 Biosphere Phases

BioSearch classifies planets into five evolutionary phases:

---

## 🔵 Phase 0 — Prebiotic / Inactive World

No stable biosphere conditions.

* No persistent oceans or highly unstable environments
* No meaningful biosignature formation
* Pre-life or barren planetary conditions

---

## 🔥 Phase 1 — Hydrothermal Biosphere (Barberton Analog)

Early microbial ecosystems driven by geothermal energy.

* Strong hydrothermal activity
* Localized water-rock interaction systems
* Primitive microbial habitability
* Early biosignature formation in silica-rich environments

> Earth analog: Barberton Greenstone Belt (~3.5 Ga)

---

## 🌊 Phase 2 — Marine Microbial Biosphere

Stable oceans supporting widespread microbial life.

* Persistent liquid oceans
* Moderate geothermal activity
* Microbial mats and biofilms
* Strong sedimentary biosignature preservation

> Earth analog: early Archean marine ecosystems

---

## ⚡ Phase 3 — Oxygenated / Transitional Biosphere (Transvaal Analog)

Atmospheric transformation phase.

* Increasing oxygen or oxidizing atmosphere
* Enhanced metabolic diversity
* Strong global geochemical cycling
* Improved detectability of biosignatures

> Earth analog: Transvaal Basin oxygenation events

---

## 🌱 Phase 4 — Terrestrial Biosphere

Surface-based ecosystems dominate.

* Stable landmasses
* Fluvial and terrestrial microbial systems
* Strong interaction between biosphere and atmosphere
* Variable preservation due to erosion and tectonics

> Earth analog: late Archean–early Proterozoic transition

---

# 📊 Model Outputs

BioSearch produces three key metrics:

## 1. Emergence Score

Measures how strongly planetary conditions support life emergence.

## 2. Preservation Probability

Estimates how likely biosignatures are to survive geological processes.

## 3. Detectability Score

Estimates how observable biosignatures would be from remote sensing.

---

# 🧮 Final Classification

Each planet receives:

* **Total BioSearch Score**
* **Assigned Biosphere Phase (0–4)**

These outputs represent:

> the evolutionary stage and observational accessibility of a planet’s biosphere.

---

# 🧬 Calibration Systems

BioSearch is grounded in Earth analog datasets:

## 🏞️ Barberton Greenstone Belt

* Early hydrothermal microbial ecosystems
* Primitive biosignature formation environments

## 🌍 Transvaal Basin

* Atmospheric oxygen transition system
* Increased biosignature detectability phase

These provide empirical constraints for phase classification.

---

# 🪐 Intended Use

BioSearch is designed for:

* Exoplanet habitability classification
* Biosignature detection likelihood ranking
* Planetary evolution modeling
* Astrobiology education and simulation
* Earth analog comparative studies

---

# ⚠️ Scientific Note

BioSearch is a **phase inference model**, not a life detection system.

It evaluates:

> the likelihood and visibility of biosignatures given planetary evolution states.
